How Picks Are Made
Four factors combine to produce a 0–100 confidence score for each prop.
① L10 vs Season Delta
The gap between a player's last 10 games average and their full-season average. A player averaging 24 pts in L10 vs 19 pts for the season is in clear hot form. This is the strongest signal — recent form predicts near-term performance far better than season averages.
② Trend Consistency
Not just the average — we measure how many of the last 10 games beat the season average. A player who beat their season avg in 8 of 10 games scores much higher than one who had one huge outlier game that pulled the average up.
③ Stat Reliability
Points are the most consistent stat for featured players — they look for their shot regardless of game flow. Rebounds depend on matchup and pace. Assists are the most volatile — one defensive adjustment disrupts them. We apply reliability weights: PTS +8, REB +5, AST +2.
④ Minutes Floor
Only players averaging ≥22 mins/game in the L10 qualify. Small-sample blowout minutes or injury returns distort averages. The minutes floor ensures we're evaluating real rotation players with consistent opportunity.
What would make this more accurate: Adding opponent defensive ranking vs stat type (e.g. "opponent allows 3rd-most PTS to SGs"), home/away splits, back-to-back fatigue flags, and injury reports. These require a paid data provider like BallDontLie or SportsDataIO. The current model is strong on form but blind to matchup context.
